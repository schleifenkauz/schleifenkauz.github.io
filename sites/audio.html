Mixed acoustic-electronic music

Rondo is a work for clarinet, string trio, conductor, and live electronics. The ensemble is miked throughout the performance, and at moments defined in the score, specified segments of live- captured sound are electronically transformed and reintroduced through loudspeakers. The clarinet introduces a theme that recurs twice as a refrain, each time undergoing electronic transformation. This material is contrasted with three-note clarinet multiphonics, which are subsequently imitated by the string trio. Fragments from the clarinet's melody recur throughout the piece as contrapuntal interjections. The central section takes the form of a fugue, in which the ensemble and the computer take turns presenting the subject. Throughout the piece, the electronically transformed sounds are tightly interwoven with the acoustic material, requiring precise synchronization between the ensemble and the computer. Instead of the ensemble being beholden to a fixed digital click track, the computer follows the flexible pulse indicated by the conductor, which already functions as a shared temporal reference for the human performers. The conductor's gestures are captured by a camera and analyzed in real- time to infer tempo information, enabling precise coordination without constraining the performers' freedom to shape musical time expressively.
