Ponticello

Ponticello is a composition environment for electronic music based on the audio programming language SuperCollider.
It grew out of a need I face when composing with SuperCollider. I really like the versatility and conciseness of audio programming and that this compositional medium guides my view on an emerging piece in a way that is completely different from traditional musical notation. But there are a lot of cases where I want to look at a piece of music not as a text-file, but as a graphical score, with musical time flowing from left to right, simulatenous events being at the same horizontal position, while retaining the versatility of SuperColliders approach to musical organization.
Ponticello attempts to deliver a useful bridge between the world of text-based audio programming and the intuitivity of graphical score representations, utilizing and combining concepts from both notational paradigmas.

Installation

Ponticello uses the SuperCollider platform for audio synthesis. It can be downloaded here.
Ponticello can be installed by executing one of the following code snippets in the shell, depending on your operating system.
(An appropriate Java Runtime Environment and the JavaFX SDK are both automatically downloaded if you don't have them installed on your system.)

Windows (PowerShell)


Invoke-WebRequest "https://schleifenkauz.de/software/get-ponticello.ps1" -UseBasicParsing | iex

Linux/Mac (Bash)


curl -fsSL "https://schleifenkauz.de/software/get-ponticello.sh" | bash
If the installation process fails on your system, you can contact me here for help.
